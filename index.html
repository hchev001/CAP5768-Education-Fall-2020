Have you wondered the worth of your education?

Maybe you've asked yourself if what you are majoring in is worth it?

Or if what degrees you have attained have provided you with satisfaction or return on investment?



Follow this presentation as we explore various datasets that help us answer the following questions:

  • How is educational attainment related to income and to career satisfaction in the United States?

  • How is income and career satisfaction stratified by degree and program?

  • What role do University rankings play in satisfaction with education received?

Role of Field of education in Career Satisfaction



People from different fields of education have deifferent career satisfaction.

The field of education graph shows that - people who work in the field of business, management, education are more satisfied with their careers and people from life science, health related fields are less satisfied; regardless of how much they earn.

The Main Reasons for Career Dissatisfaction

People's opinion about changing the existing factors that may lead them to career satisfaction.

Factors:

  • Change field

  • Change school

  • Pursue more degrees

  • Pursue less degrees

  • Pursue no degrees


If given a chance to change one thing that will lead people to the career satisfaction, 41% of people chose to pursue more degrees whereas, just 3% chose not to pursue any degree.

Returns on Investment

Pie chart obtained from the cost/benefit survey on 6297 sample population, belonging to different education levels and varied fields.


On comparing the cost and benefit of pursuing degrees, 86% of the sample population think that they are getting positive returns on investment and in that 43% responded that they are getting much larger benefits.

University Ranking Influence to People's Satisfaction With Their Career and Income



Originally, the team assumed University Ranking contributes to people's satisfaction regarding their careers and income.

However, the analysis on the datasets demonstrated a fact that while thinking in general about people's satisfaction to their careers and income, it is not necessary to take university ranking into account.

FIU Undergraduates Are Consistently Highly Satisfied With Their Degrees at Graduation



The previous conclusion is prevelant in the FIU survey as well, that University rankings don't really seem to matter to gradautes' satisfaction with their degrees.

Satisfaction with quality of education at FIU has hovered at 85-86% respondents strongly agreeing/agreeing on the overall value of the university experience. These percentages do not move up (or down) as FIU’s ranking has improved over the past 10 years.

Satisfaction with FIU Undergraduate Degree



The satisfaction with FIU Undergraduate degrees has hovered around 85% for the past 10 years.

Take-Home Messages

Finish a degree, more is better (Masters or PhD). You will earn well with your degree, similar to self employed individuals if not better. Moreover, people with higher degrees are more satisfied with their careers for longer time.

In our analysis, University rankings did not make a significant difference in graduates’ overall satisfaction with the degree and expectation of income and career satisfaction. Perhaps rankings figure more into applicants’, rather than graduates’, decision-making process.
